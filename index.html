How the maths works

  • Fuel = (miles ÷ MPG) × 4.546 × £/litre (UK gallon).
  • Electricity = (miles ÷ miles-per-kWh) × a blended £/kWh from your home / public / solar charging mix — solar miles can be near-free.
  • Cumulative cost = running costs + depreciation (reducing-balance) + any one-off repairs. Buying a car isn't treated as an instant loss — it becomes an asset that then depreciates, which is what makes "keep vs switch" a fair comparison.
  • Break-even = the year the switch's cumulative cost drops below keeping your current car.

Why solar changes everything here

If you charge largely from home solar, the marginal cost of those miles is close to zero — so an EV's running cost can fall to 2–3p/mile against 14–20p/mile for petrol or diesel. Set your realistic home / public / solar split per car to see it.

Defaults & sources

Starting figures are illustrative UK 2026 values (petrol ≈ £1.40/L, home electricity ≈ 27p/kWh, rapid public ≈ 79p/kWh) drawn from the TCO calculators surveyed in this project's research notes — HSBC, The EV Pros, SmartMoneyTools, UK Finance Toolkit, EV Calculator UK, Calks and others.
